DNA Analysis Project

Introduction/Background

We did an investigation at ESUMS about DNA Analysis. Our goal was to use see which other 9th grades we were most related to using DNA extraction, PCR amplification, and Gel Elctrophoresis.

Whar We Did

First we started with DNA extraction, where we got at least one copy of each of our DNA's. Next we added in the regeants, D1S80 Primer Mix and 2X EZ PCR Master Mix™️, into our DNA. Our next step was PCR Amplification, where we made billions of copies of our DNA of interest, in our experiment was a VNTR. Then we moved onto Gel Electrophoresis. In gel electrophoresis, electricity is conducted through the agarose gel and the liquid buffer that surrounds it to seperate molecules. We then measured the DNA copies on Adobe Photoshop based on length to discover who we matched with. The in teams, we created a documentary consisting of initial and final interviews, b-roll of the project, and a stop motion animation. Is Race Biologically Real?

Race is not biologically real but people of the same race are more likely to have similarities than others. In class, we swabbed our cheeks and then extracted the DNA. We did a genetic testing with Gel Electrophoresis to see who our allele repeats matched closest with. We checked the DNA band size to find the number of repeats on our two alleles. I matched with 8 people of a different ethnicity. Out of 54 people, 28 people had matched with 0 people of their own ethnicity. The average matches of people who matched their own ethnicity was .56. Furthermore, we did an in class survey to see how the physical traits varied of our students. We raised our hands for being able to roll your tongue, lactose intolerance, left handed, and having dimples. The data showed that no one ethnicity all raised their hands at the same time. However, the maps show that people born in certain regions are more likely to have similar traits. This is because people’s ancestors lived in those regions and thus adapted to their regions creating genetic variation. A biological race is defined as a distinct, geographic population within a single species that shows genetic and physical differences from other populations of that same species. Some think that when people say race, they mean an entirely different subspecies or the phenotypes of a specific population. I think that race is the genetic variation caused by our ancestors adapting to their respective regions. People of a same race are likely to have similar traits (both phenotically and genetically, as shown on the maps) If biological race was real, then we would have observed people of the same race/ethnicity matching only each other in our experiment. However, this did not happen since all humans share 99.9% of the same DNA.
